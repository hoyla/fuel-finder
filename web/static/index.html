How outlier exclusion works

Averages on the dashboard exclude prices that fall far outside the normal range for each fuel type. We use the Tukey IQR (interquartile range) method, a standard statistical technique:

  1. For each fuel type, compute Q1 (25th percentile) and Q3 (75th percentile) of current prices
  2. Calculate IQR = Q3 − Q1 (the middle 50% spread)
  3. Any price below Q1 − 1.5 × IQR or above Q3 + 1.5 × IQR is an outlier
  4. Prices already flagged by anomaly detection (above) are also excluded

This adapts automatically to the current price distribution — thresholds aren't hardcoded. Common reasons for outlier prices include decimal-point errors (e.g. 1.50 instead of 150.0), stations submitting prices in pounds instead of pence, and stale or test data. The outlier prices are never deleted — they remain in the database and are shown below for full transparency.
